Pros & Cons – Two Approaches

Strategic trade-offs for each implementation mode
Option 1
Values
Pros
+
Aligned with Clarity AI's core proposition — sustainability, ethics and impact as the product backbone
+
More data leverage — broader set of signals (spending, behaviour, preferences) can enrich value profiling
+
Higher defensibility — tied to Clarity's proprietary impact data and scoring engine
Cons
−
Values are harder to detect from transactions — mapping spending to abstract principles requires complex inference
−
Higher friction onboarding — questionnaire-based entry adds steps and may reduce activation rate
Option 2
Communities
Pros
+
Easier onboarding — no questionnaire needed, communities are auto-detected and suggested to users
+
Communities are easier to identify — lifestyle patterns (dining, sports, parenting) are clearly visible in transaction data
+
Higher engagement potential — identity-based communities drive stronger affinity and repeat usage
Cons
−
Dilutes Clarity AI's brand — moves away from sustainability and impact, may confuse positioning
−
Uncomfortable for some banks — lifestyle segmentation may feel intrusive or off-brand for certain client profiles
−
Lower barriers to entry — generic lifestyle perks can be replicated by PFM/VFM competitors without Clarity's data edge
